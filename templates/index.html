Squirrel Detector

This project was originally designed to keep squirrels away from bird feeders. There are countless gadgets and gizmos that claim to do this but most of them are ineffective.

The idea is to use a camera to detect the presence of a squirrel and then activate a harmless deterrent. The deterrent in my case is a toy water gun that is activated by a relay. Birds or people can safely traverse the area, but squirrels will get wet. Of course, the relay could trigger anything else that you may prefer to use, such as playing a loud noise or flashing a bright light.

There are 4 main steps to this project:

  1. Collect images of squirrels in your own environment
  2. Annotate the images with bounding boxes
  3. Train a model with the collected data
  4. Detect squirrels and trigger a relay

The main reason for incorporating all of these steps into a single project was to provide a learning experience. I also wanted to capture images in my own yard to make detection more reliable. This approach lets me use the same code for training and detection.

Each step is dependant on the previous step being completed, so you must complete the steps in the order they are presented. For additional details, see the help page.

This project was designed specifically for an Nvidia Jetson Nano. Most of the code should work fine on another platform with the exception of the training section and the relay code, but I have only tested this with the Nano.
